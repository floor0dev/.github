## Our Approach

**All good software sucks**. Shitty code, tech debt, bugs... Warts are unavoidable when building a large and complex product that solves real problems.

In our experience, however, there are a few load-bearing aspects that make a codebase **resilient to these defects**. It's like the old story of warplanes: reinforce the critical parts, and a few bullet holes here can simply be brushed off:

1. **Domain Model**: The conceptual representation of your business. This is the part that coding agents most often neglect. A good domain model makes new features feel like natural extensions of the product, rather than ill-fitting patches.
2. **Architecture**: How the software is organized: the parts it's divided into, and the responsibilities of each part. Good architecture keeps your software from becoming an unworkable mess as it grows.
3. **Setup and Deployment**: The set of tools and practices used to build your product and put it in the hands of your users. It's one of the most important aspects for AI-assisted development, giving an agent strict guardrails and the ability to self-check and self-evaluate.
4. **Observability**: CCTV for your software. Shit happens, and good observability makes the difference between you pinpointing the exact cause in a matter of minutes and fixing it before users even notice, and you learning about it hours later from social media.
